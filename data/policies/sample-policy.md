# 📑 Project: VoltEdge Hardware Warranty & Service Policies

## 1. General Limited Warranty

**Coverage Period:** 24 months from the date of activation or 26 months from the date of manufacture, whichever occurs first.

**Scope:** VoltEdge warrants that the hardware components (The "Product") will be free from material defects in workmanship and materials under "Normal Use."

**Normal Use Defined:** * Operation within the temperature range of -20°C to 50°C.

* Installation by a certified VoltEdge technician or an authorized third-party partner.
* Connection to a grounded power source with surge protection (minimum 3000 Joules).

---

## 2. Specific Component Coverage

| Component | Coverage Term | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Main Logic Board** | 24 Months | Full replacement. |
| **LED Display / Touchscreen** | 12 Months | Only covers dead pixels (>5) or total backlight failure. |
| **Charging Cable / Connector** | 6 Months | Covers internal wiring failure; excludes external sheath cuts. |
| **External Housing/Casing** | 36 Months | Covers structural cracks; excludes cosmetic scratches/fading. |

---

## 3. Standard Exclusions (The "No-Go" List)

The warranty is strictly **void** under the following conditions:

1. **Unauthorized Tampering:** Any evidence of the internal seal being broken or screws being replaced by non-authorized parts.
2. **Environmental Abuse:** Damage caused by submersion in liquid, fire, or "Acts of God" (lightning strikes are only covered if an approved VoltEdge surge protector was used).
3. **Cosmetic Damage:** Scratches, dents, or discoloration that do not impact the functional performance of the device.
4. **Software Interfacing:** Failures caused by the installation of third-party firmware or unauthorized mobile app integrations.
5. **Improper Maintenance:** Build-up of "extreme debris" or insect infestation within the cooling vents.

---

## 4. Claim Validation Requirements

To successfully process a claim through the Automated Agent, the user must provide:

* **Proof of Purchase:** A clear image of the original receipt or a digital invoice.
* **Visual Evidence:** At least one (1) high-resolution photo of the damage.
* **Serial Number:** A photo of the barcode/serial number located on the underside of the unit.
